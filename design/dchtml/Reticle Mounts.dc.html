Mount rules
1 · Reticles mount on the PART, never the figure.
Center the reticle on the aimed PART's rect — figures.<id>.parts.<part>.rect in layout.json, scaled by the figure's slot scale. The figure's bbox center is never a target. No boxes, highlights, or hitbox outlines are ever drawn.

2 · Size follows the part, clamped.
reticle box = part rect's larger side × ~1.5, clamped to 64–136 screen px, so a head lock and a leg lock read the same weight.

3 · One look per FSM state (§8).
FOCUS (red brackets, PULSING — the engine cycles authored frames focus_p0→p1→p2 on the fixed tick) = locked target · AIMING (red, dashed) = THE CURSOR while a technique actively targets: clicking the technique tile replaces the pointer with this reticle until you click a body part (lock) or right-click (cancel) · SECONDARY (faint) = another module's kept target (AUTO on).

4 · The aim tag reads the HOTKEY NUMBER; the card carries the same number.
The tag stack above the reticle reads each aiming technique's hotkey number — several actives on a shared part stack their numbers. Every action-bar card shows its hotkey chip (1–N); that number is the whole wire, no part-name tag on the card.
